# Acknowledging TRINITY

If you use TRINITY in any published work, please cite the TRINITY method
paper, Teh et al. (2026),
[arXiv:2605.27517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27517) (BibTeX via
[ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2026arXiv260527517T/abstract)).

## Legacy citations

TRINITY builds on the WARPFIELD framework. Users may also wish to cite:

- Rahner, D., Pellegrini, E. W., Klessen, R. S., & Glover, S. C. O. (2017),
  *MNRAS*, 470, 4453 — WARPFIELD 1.0.
  ([ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2017MNRAS.470.4453R))
- Rahner, D., Pellegrini, E. W., Glover, S. C. O., & Klessen, R. S. (2019),
  *MNRAS*, 483, 2547 — WARPFIELD 2.0.
  ([ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2019MNRAS.483.2547R))

Stellar feedback rates are drawn by default from
[Starburst99](https://www.stsci.edu/science/starburst99/) (Leitherer et
al. 1999); any stellar-population-synthesis table can be supplied instead
(see [Parameter Specifications](?view=docs&page=parameters), *Stellar
feedback (SPS)*).
